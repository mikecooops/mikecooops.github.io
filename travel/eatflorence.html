Eat: Florence


"Bistecca alla Fiorentina"

The name literally translates to "Florentine steak", however don't be
fooled by its familiar sounding American counterpart. This steak is
cooked one way: seared on outside and rare on the inside. It is often
seasoned with salt, pepper, a hint of lemon and cooked over roasted
chestnuts for a smokiness.



"Papardelle al Cinghiale"

This is a dish made with long, wide, flat pasta and meat. "Cinghiale"
translates to wild boar, however the dish is often made with other
meats such as rabbit. The sauce tends to be on the heavier side so
you can expect it to be quite filling!



"Lampredotto"

A common folk food stemming from Florence's medieval age, the
"lampredotto" can either be served as a sandwich or on a plate. If
it's in sandwich form, it's filled with thinly sliced tripe, or intestines.
Have no fear because these intestines have been been both boiled
and seasoned. You also have the choice of either red or green sauce.

Eat: Venice


"Sarde in saor"

Although considered to be an appetizer or as the italians say "antipasto",
this dish is rich in flavor and will certainly fill up the hungry traveler who
is willing to expand his/ her pallet. Essentially, the plate is lined with
sardine fillets marinated in vinegar, onions, raisins and pine nuts.



"Risotto al nero di seppia"

With rice being a staple in the region, it makes sense to include a dish
that confirms just that. Because Venice is also known as the "city on water",
many of the native dishes include some form of sea life. The risotto's color
comes from squid, wine, onion, tomato and ink braise.



"Fritole"

These sweet pastry fritters are commonly eaten during the "Carnevale" period,
a masquerade festival and party you wouldn't want to miss. "Fritoles" are made
with a batter consisting of flour, eggs, butter, milk, sugar, pine nuts and raisins.
They are then are molded into balls and deep fried in oil and dusted with a light
coat of sugar.
